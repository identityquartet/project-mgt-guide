Why it matters

Projects rarely fail because of bad ideas.

They fail because of poor planning and execution. For small organisations with tight resources, the cost of a project that drifts can be severe — and the gains from getting it right are disproportionately large.

The cost of poor project management

  • Wasted time and money
  • Missed deadlines and commitments
  • Frustrated teams and volunteers
  • Reduced impact on the people you serve

The upside of doing it well

  • Stay focused on what actually matters
  • Use limited resources effectively
  • Spot and manage risks before they bite
  • Deliver results with confidence
The project journey

How a project moves from idea to impact.

Every project follows a simple journey. Understanding each stage helps small businesses and charities plan better, reduce risk, and deliver stronger results.

Stage 01

Initiate

Define the idea, purpose, and value of the project.
Stage 02

Plan

Set goals, scope, timeline, responsibilities, and resources.
Stage 03

Deliver

Carry out the work and keep the project moving.
Stage 04

Monitor

Track progress, manage risks, and adjust when needed.
Stage 05

Close & Learn

Review results, capture lessons, and improve future projects.
